Kyo-Uchiwa

The Gentle Breeze of the Capital. Discover the breathtaking traditional fans handcrafted in Kyoto—a perfect harmony of historical artisan techniques and minimalist modern beauty.

Heritage & Craft

Miyako Uchiwa:
The Capital's Masterpiece

Kyo-Uchiwa, historically known as "Miyako Uchiwa" (Capital Fan), carries the elegant aesthetic of Japan's ancient capital. Crafted by the dedicated artisans of the Kyoto Fan Commerce and Industry Cooperative, these fans represent the pinnacle of traditional technique inherited over generations.

Since 1944, craftsmen at Shiomi Uchiwa have been seamlessly inserting delicate radial bamboo ribs piece by piece, layering pristine washi paper to create a fan that is not only functional but a true piece of art. Every breeze it creates tells a story of Kyoto's dedication to quality.

The Artisan Process

Generations of Handcrafted Perfection

Every single Kyo-Uchiwa is born from meticulous manual labor. From the precise splitting of bamboo ribs to the delicate application of washi paper, these techniques have remained beautifully unchanged.

A Legacy Preserved

The profound atmosphere of traditional Kyoto workshops, where time-honored techniques are faithfully protected and passed down.

Delicate Handwork

Artisans spend years mastering the blade, hand-slicing a single piece of bamboo into dozens of hair-thin ribs (hone) that form the fan's exquisite skeleton.

Modern Living

A Beautiful Addition
To Any Space

While rooted deeply in traditional Japanese kimono culture, Kyo-Uchiwa are designed for today’s lifestyle. Their minimalist design and exquisite materials make them breathtaking interior accents.

Whether perfectly placed on a sleek wooden console or gracefully cooling you on a warm summer evening, Kyo-Uchiwa bridges the gap between ancient craft and modern luxury living. It is a stunning, deeply cultural gift to share with friends and loved ones.
