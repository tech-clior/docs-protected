The Language of the Earth

Shigaraki-yaki: One of Japan's Six Ancient Kilns

With a history spanning over 1,200 years, Shigaraki (located in modern Shiga Prefecture) is revered as one of Japan's Six Ancient Kilns. Unlike highly decorated porcelain, Shigaraki-yaki embraces Wabi-Sabi—finding beauty in the raw, natural texture of the earth.

The Language of Fire

Shigaraki-yaki is famous for Hiiro (fire colors)—beautiful scarlet and burnt orange gradients that appear naturally during kiln firing. Combined with falling ash glazes (vidro), no two pieces ever leave the kiln looking exactly the same.

Warmth and Texture

Using coarse, sandy clay gathered from the bed of ancient Lake Biwa, the ceramics possess a porous, textured signature. This earthy warmth gives Shigaraki pieces a profound presence in any modern interior space.

400 Years of Heritage

Meizan Kiln: Absolute Master Craftsmanship

Nestled within Shigaraki lies the historic Meizan Kiln, a lineage of ceramicists holding strong to centuries of tradition.

100% Handcrafted Dedication

For over 400 years, the artisans at Meizan have respected the spirit of the earth, carrying forward secrets of clay, fire, and ash that cannot be written down, but only felt through generations of hands.

In an age heavily dominated by mass automation and slip-casting molds, the ceramicists at Meizan strongly refuse to compromise. Every single piece is completely handcrafted by Master Artisans. The shaping of the clay on the wheel, the delicate application of glaze, and the meticulous, exhausting process of wood-firing are done entirely by human touch.

This unrelenting human touch is what gives Meizan Kiln products—from large Ikebana vases to their famous miniature "Hoccori" healing owl figures—their soulful resonance.
